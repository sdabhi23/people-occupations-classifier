Isaac "Ike" Barinholtz (born February 18, 1977) is an American comedian, actor and screenwriter. He was a cast member on MADtv from 2002 to 2007, Eastbound & Down (2012), and had a regular role on The Mindy Project. In his film work, he is best known for his acting roles in Neighbors (2014 film) Neighbors (2014) and its sequel, Neighbors 2: Sorority Rising (2016), Sisters (2015 film) Sisters (2015), Suicide Squad (film) Suicide Squad (2016) and Blockers (film) Blockers (2018), as well for as co-writing the screenplay for the 2016 comedy film Central Intelligence.

==Early life and education==
Barinholtz was born in the Rogers Park, Chicago Rogers Park area of Chicago, Illinois, and was raised in its Lake View, Chicago Lake View neighborhood,<ref name="ChicagoTribune-2012">{{cite news last=Metz first=Nina url=http://www.chicagotribune.com/entertainment/ct-ent-0912-fall-preview-tv-profile-20120911,0,4096500.story title=Dork and Mindy: Chicagoan Ike Barinholtz ready to throw his weight around on new project date=2012-09-11 publisher=chicagotribune.com accessdate=2013-02-10}}</ref> the son of Peggy and Alan Barinholtz, an attorney.<ref>{{cite news last=Sullivan first=Catey url=http://nl.newsbank.com/nl-search/we/Archives?p_product=PGHB&p_multi=SKYB&p_theme=pghb&p_action=search&p_maxdocs=200&p_topdoc=1&p_text_direct-0=118DF2518B7E4E78&p_field_direct-0=document_id&p_perpage=10&p_sort=YMD_date:D&s_trackval=GooglePM title= 	Funny sons of funny fathers date=2007-04-25 publisher=Chicago Sun-Times accessdate=2013-02-10}}</ref> Barinholtz has described his parents as "liberal people with great senses of humor," and has said that he was raised in a "very funny home."<ref name="ChicagoTribune-2004">{{cite news last=Isackson first=Noah url=http://articles.chicagotribune.com/2004-04-04/features/0404040303_1_funny-people-chicago-improv-festival-sports-bloopers title=Ike Barinholtz - Mad About Tv Comedy date=2004-04-04 publisher=chicagotribune.com accessdate=2013-02-10}}</ref> Barinholtz is Jewish,<ref>{{cite web last=Radloff  first=Jessica  url=http://www.glamour.com/entertainment/blogs/obsessed/2014/09/ike-barinholtz-mindy-project  title=Ike Barinholtz From The Mindy Project Talks Chris Messina, Mindy Kaling, and Marriage  publisher=Glamour  date=  accessdate=2016-05-23}}</ref> and attended Bernard Zell Anshe Emet Day School, before attending The Latin School of Chicago for high school. He had planned on becoming a politician,<ref name="ChicagoTribune-2004" /> but decided to move to Los Angeles, California, to be an actor instead. He worked at a telemarketing company and as a busboy before starting a career in comedy.<ref name="RedEye-2013">{{cite news last=Wagner first=Curt url=http://articles.redeyechicago.com/2013-01-07/entertainment/36197592_1_lou-malnati-deep-dish-ike-barinholtz/2 title=Ike Barinholtz finds time for laughs in The Mindy Project date=2013-01-07 publisher=redeyechicago.com accessdate=2013-02-10}}</ref>

Barinholtz also attended Boston University and after dropping out, he worked for the Chicago Transit Authority before starting his comedy career.<ref name="ChicagoTribune-2012" /> In a 2013 interview, he said he dropped out of school because he hated it and was doing poorly.<ref name="RedEye-2013" />

In a 2012 phone interview, Barinholtz said he was inspired to pursue a career in comedy after attending a comedy show at The Vic Theatre and seeing several other comedians.<ref name="ChicagoTribune-2012" /> Afterwards, he started taking comedy classes at The Second City, ImprovOlympic and Annoyance Theatre, and was not successful at first, but chose to stick with it.<ref name="RedEye-2013" /> His younger brother Jon Barinholtz is also an actor and comedian.

==Career==
Barinholtz spent two years in Amsterdam with the famed comedy troupe Boom Chicago, along with Jordan Peele, Josh Meyers (actor) Josh Meyers and Nicole Parker. Barinholtz hosted the Worst of Boom Night during a 10-year anniversary of the improv troupe Boom Chicago, where they performed their worst material from previous shows.

He has appeared in the films The Adventures of Big Handsome Guy and His Little Friend (2005), Love, Fear and Rabbits (2005) and Down (2001). Barinholtz currently lives in Los Angeles. Barinholtz is a founding member of The Lindbergh Babies, an improv group directed by Del Close, who worked with John Belushi, Bill Murray, Mike Myers (actor) Mike Myers and Chris Farley.

In 2012, Barinholtz joined the cast of the third season of the HBO series Eastbound & Down, playing the role of Ivan Dochenko, Kenny Powers Russian rival.

He starred alongside Seth Rogen in the 2014 comedy Neighbors (2014 film) Neighbors, as well as its 2016 Neighbors 2: Sorority Rising sequel. Also in 2016, Barinholtz played prison guard Griggs in the DC Comics film Suicide Squad (film) Suicide Squad.

Barinholtz appeared in a regular role as nurse Morgan Tookers on the Hulu comedy series The Mindy Project. He was also a writer and story editor on the series. He also has a role in Friends from College, a Netflix original.

===MADtv===
Barinholtz officially joined the cast of MADtv in 2002 as a feature performer for the eighth season. He was later promoted to a repertory performer status the following season. Barinholtz would often pair up with fellow cast member Josh Meyers (actor) Josh Meyers in various sketches for the eighth and ninth seasons similar to the Mike Myers (actor) Mike Myers/Dana Carvey and Jimmy Fallon/Horatio Sanz duos on Saturday Night Live. Barinholtz also paired up with fellow cast member Bobby Lee in some of his sketches in the later seasons. Some of his famous characters included Abercrombie & Fitch model Dutch and Principal Lankenstein from the Coach Hines sketches.

Barinholtz performs celebrity impressions, including Alex Trebek, Andy Dick, Arnold Schwarzenegger, Ashton Kutcher, Bo Bice, Dane Cook, Felicity Huffman, Fidel Castro, Howard Dean, Howie Long, Kevin Federline, Mark Wahlberg, Matt Damon, Nick Lachey, Nick Nolte, Pat Sajak, and Ralph Nader.

In 2007, he decided not to renew his contract for the 13th season of MADtv. His contract, like most other MADtv cast members, was for five seasons which was up for renewal at the conclusion of the shows 12th season. Barinholtz has commented that he started getting "restless" and did not see eye to eye with some of the MADtv bosses about how decisions were being made.<ref name="RedEye-2013" />

In 2009, he performed at the List of improvisational theater festivals Chicago Improv Festival alongside former MADtv cast member Jordan Peele.<ref>{{cite news last=Jones first=Chris url=http://articles.chicagotribune.com/2009-02-18/entertainment/0902170225_1_headliners-chicago-improv-festival-cuts title=Chicago Improv Festival announces headliners, cuts prices date=2009-02-18 publisher=chicagotribune.com accessdate=2013-02-10}}</ref> In 2012, Barinholtz performed at another Chicago Improv Festival event with his brother, Jon.<ref>{{cite news last=Wagner first=Curt url=http://articles.redeyechicago.com/2012-03-25/entertainment/31237516_1_chicago-improv-festival-jordan-peele-keegan-michael-key title=Chicago Improv Festival to honor Key and Peele date=2012-03-25 publisher=redeyechicago.com accessdate=2013-02-10}}</ref>

He has frequently worked with comedian Dave Stassen, with whom he also went to high school.<ref name="ChicagoTribune-2012" /> They co-wrote a SPIKE pilot in which Barinholtz also starred, Mega Winner,<ref>{{cite news last=Goldberg first=Lesley url=http://www.hollywoodreporter.com/live-feed/eastbound-down-mad-tv-alum-230408 title=Eastbound & Down: Mad TV Alum Takes the Mound (Exclusive) date=2011-09-01 publisher=hollywoodreporter.com accessdate=2013-02-10}}</ref> and the 2016 film Central Intelligence.<ref>{{cite news last=Lussier first=Germain url=http://collider.com/galaxy-quest-director-dean-parisot-to-helm-helms-comedy-central-intelligence/ title=GALAXY QUEST Director Dean Parisot to Helm Helms Comedy CENTRAL INTELLIGENCE date=2010-02-25 publisher=collider.com accessdate=2013-02-10}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Barinholtz is married to Erica Hanson, with whom he has three daughters.<ref>{{cite web last=Harp  first=Justin  url=http://www.digitalspy.com/celebrity/news/a490742/ike-barinholtz-wife-erica-hanson-welcome-daughter.html  title=Ike Barinholtz, wife Erica Hanson welcome daughter  website=Digitalspy.com  date=2013-06-17  accessdate=2015-12-16}}</ref><ref>{{cite web url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=FwIBoO_kgLE  title=Mindy Kaling on The Talk  publisher=YouTube  date=  accessdate=2015-12-16}}</ref>

Ike’s brother is fellow actor/comedian Jon Barinholtz.

Barinholtz was shooting his film Blockers (film) Blockers when he suffered an accident during a fall stunt. The accident left him with two fractured cervical vertebrae in his neck.
