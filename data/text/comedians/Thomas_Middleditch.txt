Thomas Steven Middleditch<ref>{{cite web url=https://variety.com/2016/tv/features/thomas-middleditch-patrick-stewart-silicon-valley-blunt-talk-1201794682/ title=Thomas Middleditch and Patrick Stewart on Doing Standup, Nicknames and Crazy Fan Encounters website=Variety (magazine) Variety first=Debra last=Birnbaum date=June 14, 2016}}</ref> (born March 10, 1982) is a Canadian actor and television writer, known for his role as Richard Hendricks in the HBO series Silicon Valley (TV series) Silicon Valley, for which he was nominated for a Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Lead Actor in a Comedy Series. He voiced Harold Hutchins in Captain Underpants: The First Epic Movie (2017). Middleditch also appears in ads for Verizon Wireless.<ref>{{cite web url=https://www.fiercewireless.com/wireless/top-5-wireless-ads-t-mobile-spends-almost-30m-netflix-ads title=The top 5 wireless ads: T-Mobile spends almost $30M on Netflix ads website=FierceWireless.com first=Mike last=Dano date=November 14, 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite web url=https://www.nytimes.com/2017/04/21/arts/television/thomas-middleditch-of-silicon-valley-on-doing-verizon-ads-and-flying-his-own-plane.html title=Thomas Middleditch of Silicon Valley on Doing Verizon Ads and Flying His Own Plane website=The New York Times first=Dave last=Itzkoff date=April 21, 2017}}</ref>

==Early life==
Middleditch was born and raised in Nelson, British Columbia.<ref name="BA">{{cite web title=BA #085: Thomas Middleditch url=http://boxangeles.com/2015/06/29/ba-085-thomas-middleditch/ date=June 29, 2015 website=Box Angeles podcast}}</ref> His parents are British.<ref>{{cite web url=http://www.theapiary.org/thebastion/2006/9/15/inside-with-thomas-middleditch-comedian-and-actor.html title=Inside With: Thomas Middleditch, Comedian and Actor last=McQuern first=Elizabeth date=September 15, 2006 website=TheApiary.org accessdate=June 3, 2017}}</ref> He was cast in a play in grade eight which he said "changed everything" for him. He went from an indoorsy kid who was bullied, to becoming the funny, popular kid in high school. He discovered Improvisational theatre improv in grade school from performing with Theatresports.<ref name="BA"/>

He studied at the University of Victoria before moving to Toronto, where he auditioned for and enrolled in George Brown College, but never attended classes.<ref name="BA"/> He instead opted to make sketches on his own, and worked at a New Balance store for an income.<ref name="BA"/> He then relocated to Chicago, where he took classes at The Second City and iO Theater, while also performing regularly.<ref name="BA"/> Due to immigration issues, he worked various cash jobs until Charna Halpern sponsored him to get his Work permit work visa.<ref name="BA"/> While performing on a Second City cruise, he got an audition for Saturday Night Live.<ref name="BA"/> Though he did not get cast, the routine he used in his audition landed him a network Talent holding deal holding deal and he moved to New York City.<ref name="BA"/>

==Career==
File:Thomas Middleditch at SXSW 2016 (cropped).jpg right thumb upright Middleditch at SXSW 2016
While living in New York City, Middleditch began acting in commercials. In 2007 a sketch video for Chicken McNuggets he made with Fernando Sosa in Chicago was purchased by McDonalds and used as a commercial.<ref name="BA"/><ref>{{cite web title=McNuggets McNuggets...What? url=http://adage.com/article/garfield-the-blog/mcnuggets-mcnuggets/119577/ date=July 27, 2007 website=AdAge}}</ref> He appeared in the 2009 romantic comedy Splinterheads, portraying the lead role of Justin Frost.<ref name=Splinterheads>{{cite web last=Debruge first=Peter title=Review: Splinterheads url=https://variety.com/2009/film/reviews/splinterheads-1200477598/ accessdate=February 2, 2014 website=Variety date=November 5, 2009}}</ref> On March 25, 2010, he joined a CBS pilot Hitched, written by Josh Schwartz and directed by Rob Greenberg.<ref>{{cite web last=Andreeva first=Nellie title=Two CBS pilots add cast members url=http://www.hollywoodreporter.com/hr/imdb/article_display.jsp?vnu_content_id=1004078625&vnu_special_account_code=thrsiteimdbpro accessdate=February 4, 2014 work=The Hollywood Reporter date=March 25, 2010}}</ref> On May 5, 2011, Middleditch made his first appearance in Jake and Amir, in the episode "Jake and Amir: Doobs".<ref>{{cite web url=http://www.collegehumor.com/video/6500252/jake-and-amir-doobs title=Jake and Amir: Doobs date=May 5, 2011 website=CollegeHumor accessdate=May 10, 2015}}</ref> He made several returns to the web series.

On May 31, 2011, Middleditch joined the cast of the Paramount Pictures comedy film Fun Size, directed by Josh Schwartz.<ref>{{cite web title=Johnny Knoxville, Unique Blend of Cast Join Fun Size Comedy url=http://www.bloody-disgusting.com/news/24792 accessdate=4 February 2014 website=Bloody Disgusting date=May 31, 2011}}</ref> On August 8, 2011, he joined Road to Nardo,<ref>{{cite web last=Sneider first=Jeff title=Middleditch takes Road to Nardo url=https://variety.com/2011/film/news/middleditch-takes-road-to-nardo-1118041071/ accessdate=February 4, 2014 website=Variety date=August 8, 2011}}</ref> a film that would have been Scot Armstrongs directorial debut. On December 12, 2011, he joined Jay Roachs The Campaign (film) The Campaign, starring Will Ferrell and Zach Galifianakis.<ref>{{cite web last=Sneider first=Jeff title=Middleditch in Warners team of Rivals url=https://variety.com/2011/film/news/middleditch-in-warners-team-of-rivals-1118047397/ accessdate=February 4, 2014 website=Variety date=December 12, 2011}}</ref>

On July 23, 2012, Middleditch joined the cast of the comedy film Someone Marry Barry, directed and written by Rob Pearlstein and produced by Barry Josephson.<ref>{{cite web last=Kit first=Borys title=Tyler Labine, Damon Wayans Jr., Lucy Punch Starring in Comedy Someone Marry Barry url=http://www.hollywoodreporter.com/news/tyler-labine-damon-wayans-jr-lucy-punch-353224 accessdate=February 4, 2014 work=The Hollywood Reporter date=July 23, 2012}}</ref> On August 23, 2012, he had a role in The Office (U.S. TV series) The Office{{}}s final season episode "The Farm (The Office) The Farm".<ref>{{cite web last=Andreeva first=Nellie title=Thomas Middleditch Cast As Dwights Brother On The Office Spinoff url=http://deadline.com/2012/08/thomas-middleditch-cast-as-dwights-brother-on-the-office-spinoff/ accessdate=February 4, 2014 website=Deadline.com date=August 23, 2012}}</ref> He had a role in the 2013 comedy fantasy film The Brass Teapot, directed by Ramaa Mosley,<ref>{{cite web last=Debruge first=Peter title=Film Review: The Brass Teapot url=https://variety.com/2013/film/reviews/film-review-the-brass-teapot-1200332230/ accessdate=February 4, 2014 website=Variety date=April 2, 2013}}</ref> and played a police officer in the 2013 CBS Films coming-of-age comedy film The Kings of Summer.<ref>{{cite web last=Cunningham first=Todd title=Toys House Goes to CBS Films url=http://www.thewrap.com/movies/article/sundance-2013-toys-house-goes-cbs-films-74276 accessdate=February 4, 2014 website=TheWrap date=January 22, 2013}}</ref>

On January 30, 2013, Middleditch joined HBOs comedy pilot Silicon Valley (TV series) Silicon Valley playing Richard Hendricks.<ref>{{cite web last=Andreeva first=Nellie title=T.J. Miller, Thomas Middleditch Cast In Mike Judges HBO Comedy Pilot Silicon Valley url=http://deadline.com/2013/01/t-j-miller-thomas-middleditch-cast-in-mike-judges-hbo-comedy-pilot-silicon-valley/ accessdate=February 4, 2014 website=Deadline.com date=January 30, 2013}}</ref> He has said that the pilot was written with him specifically in mind as the lead (the character was originally named Thomas Pickering; the latter being his mothers maiden name).<ref name="BA"/> This was a result of a "random" audio sketch he once made for a Stand-up comedy stand-up routine which he would later animate himself and pitched to several people, including Mike Judges production partners John Altschuler and Dave Krinsky, who would later create the show.<ref name="BA"/>

In 2014 he joined a CollegeHumor series on youtube called CAMP.

For his work on the show, Middleditch has garnered two Satellite Awards nominations and two Critics Choice Television Award nominations.<ref>{{cite web url=http://tvline.com/2015/05/31/critics-choice-television-awards-winners-2015-full-list/ title=Critics Choice Television Awards: HBO, Better Call Saul, Taraji P. On April 23, 2013, he was added to the cast of the comedy film Search Party (film) Search Party, the feature directorial debut of Scot Armstrong.<ref>{{cite web last=Kit first=Borys title=Search Party Finds Its Stars, Begins Shooting Next Week url=http://www.hollywoodreporter.com/news/search-party-finds-stars-begins-444776 accessdate=February 4, 2014 website=The Hollywood Reporter date=April 23, 2013}}</ref> Middleditch made a small cameo appearance in the 2013 film The Wolf of Wall Street (2013 film) The Wolf of Wall Street as a broker who is fired for cleaning his fish bowl during office hours.<ref>{{cite video publisher=YouTube url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=jxV2Jw1PDc0 title=The Wolf of Wall Street}}</ref>

He has played as a hipster thief in the episode "Sunday Funday" on the show Youre The Worst. On The Pete Holmes Show, he played Nightcrawler (comics) Nightcrawler and Gambit (comics) Gambit from the X-Men and Vega (Street Fighter) Vega and Ken Masters Ken from Street Fighter II. On October 16, 2013, it was announced that the Disney XD-produced animated series Penn Zero: Part-Time Hero would star Middleditch as the titular multiverse-hopping hero. The series premiered in late 2014.<ref>{{cite web url=http://www.deadline.com/2013/10/disney-xd-orders-animated-series-penn-zero-part-time-hero/ title=Disney XD Orders Animated Series Penn Zero: Part-Time Hero website=Deadline.com date=October 16, 2013}}</ref> In 2015, he co-starred in the films The Final Girls and The Bronze (film) The Bronze.<ref>{{cite web url=http://www.dreadcentral.com/news/52884/four-more-sign-on-to-tangle-with-the-final-girls-including-the-vampire-diaries-nina-dobrev/ title=Four More Sign on to Tangle with The Final Girls Including The Vampire Diaries Nina Dobrev website=Dread Central first=John last=Squires date=April 11, 2014 accessdate=April 27, 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite web url=http://www.hitfix.com/news/sebastian-stan-cant-out-fox-thomas-middleditch-on-the-bronze-red-carpet title=Sebastian Stan cant out-fox Thomas Middleditch on The Bronze red carpet date=January 22, 2015 website=HitFix}}</ref> Also in 2015, he began streaming himself playing video games on the website Twitch.tv Twitch.<ref>{{cite web url=http://www.twitch.tv/middleditch title=Twitch website=Twitch}}</ref>

Middleditch voiced Harold Hutchins in DreamWorks Animations animated feature Captain Underpants: The First Epic Movie (2017), based on Dav Pilkeys book Captain Underpants.<ref>{{cite web last=Abramovitch first=Seth title=Ed Helms, Kevin Hart Slip Into DreamWorks Animations Captain Underpants url=http://www.hollywoodreporter.com/news/ed-helms-kevin-hart-slip-673028 accessdate=February 4, 2014 website=The Hollywood Reporter date=January 21, 2014}}</ref> As of 2017, Middleditch has appeared in Verizon Communications Verizon commercials as their spokesperson.<ref>{{cite web url=https://www.nytimes.com/2017/04/21/arts/television/thomas-middleditch-of-silicon-valley-on-doing-verizon-ads-and-flying-his-own-plane.html title=Thomas Middleditch of Silicon Valley on Doing Verizon Ads and Flying His Own Plane last=Itzkoff first=Dave date=April 21, 2017 website=The New York Times accessdate=June 4, 2017}}</ref> On May 31, 2017, he was cast in the MonsterVerse film Godzilla: King of the Monsters (2019 film) Godzilla: King of the Monsters.<ref>{{cite web url=https://variety.com/2017/film/news/godzilla-sequel-thomas-middleditch-silicon-valley-1202448069/ title=Silicon Valley Star Thomas Middleditch Joins Godzilla Sequel (EXCLUSIVE) last=Kroll first=Justin website=Variety date=May 31, 2017 accessdate=May 31, 2017}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Middleditch became engaged to costume designer Mollie Gates in June 2015.<ref name=USM>{{cite web last=Webber first=Stephanie title=Silicon Valleys Thomas Middleditch Engaged to Girlfriend Mollie Gates: "Theyre Both Very Happy" url=http://www.usmagazine.com/celebrity-news/news/silicon-valleys-thomas-middleditch-engaged-to-girlfriend-mollie-gates-201566 accessdate=July 16, 2015 website=Us Weekly date=June 6, 2015}}</ref> They were married on August 22, 2015 in Middleditchs hometown.

Middleditch has described his own upbringing as agnosticism agnostic. He believed in a "classic Christianity Christian religion" at a young age. He has described himself as a Christian, but also as an atheist.<ref>{{cite podcast title=Thomas Middleditch website=Totally Laime minutes=56 date=January 11, 2013 accessdate=June 18, 2015 url=http://www.totallylaime.com/2013/01/11/thomas-middleditch/}}</ref>
