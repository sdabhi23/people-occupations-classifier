Douglas Stanhope (born March 25, 1967) is an American stand-up comedian, actor, author, and podcast host.

==Early life==
Stanhope was born on March 25, 1967 in Worcester, Massachusetts to an upper middle class family.<ref name=esquire2013/><ref name=independent2004/> His father Russ was head of science at Stanhopes school, and died in 2000. His mother Bonnie (née Kirk) was a waitress. A former alcoholic, she quit drinking and left home for Florida when Stanhope was ten following their divorce.<ref name=esquire2013>{{cite journal url=https://www.highbeam.com/doc/1G1-352848309.html title=Doug Stanhope: Scott Raab Talks to the Most Fearless Man in Comedy about Louis C.K., Meth, Charity, Jerry Seinfeld, Dr. Drew, the Desert, and Lies date=1 November 2013 magazine=Esquire first=Scott last=Raab via=Highbeam Research subscription=yes accessdate=20 July 2018}}</ref> In 1998, he convinced her to live near him in West Hollywood.<ref name=independent2004/> In her later life she suffered from emphysema. In 2008, at her request, she wished for Stanhope to be involved in an assisted suicide with a mixture of cocktails and prescription morphine. Stanhope waited until the statute of limitations had expired before telling the story in his stand-up act and his second book.<ref>{{cite web url=https://www.mlive.com/entertainment/kalamazoo/index.ssf/2013/09/comedian_doug_stanhope_roasted.html title=Comedian Doug Stanhope roasted his dying mother, but is not a Hitler fan: Dark comedian coming to District Square publisher=Michigan Live first=Mark last=Wedel date=23 September 2018 accessdate=18 July 2018}}</ref><ref>[http://www.heraldscotland.com/news/13801297.No_laughing_matter__Comic_Doug_Stanhope_tells_how_he_saved_a_man_from_jumping_off_Edinburgh_bridge Stanhope tells how he saved a man from jumping off Edinburgh bridge and discusses the death of his own emphysema-stricken mother, who killed herself ], heraldscotland.com; accessed October 3, 2015.</ref> Stanhope has an elder brother, Jeff.<ref name=apt/><ref name=independent2004/>

At age 12, Stanhopes school psychiatrist wrote a letter to his mother detailing his concern with Stanhopes tendency to sketch gory images and adult themed cartoons, suggesting he was "in need of professional help".<ref name=guardian2011>{{cite web url=https://www.theguardian.com/stage/2011/sep/10/doug-stanhope-interview-tim-jonze title=Doug Stanhope: pro-choice, pro-drugs, but defiantly anti-Guardian first=Tim last=Jonze date=9 September 2011 work=the Guardian accessdate=18 July 2018}}</ref> Stanhope quit school at fifteen.<ref name=independent2004/> Among his early comedy influences were Monty Python and National Lampoon (magazine) National Lampoon.<ref name=apt/> Before he started comedy full time, Stanhope worked as in telemarketing, "borderline legal stuff, trying to scam people basically".<ref name=guardian2011/>

==Career==
===1990s===
Stanhope began his stand-up comedy career in 1990 at age 23,<ref name=guardian2011/> having moved to Las Vegas after six months in Los Angeles. He did his first routine at an open mic night at a local bar, where it made him "sit down and write jokes".<ref name=apt>{{cite web url=http://apt613.ca/comedian-doug-stanhope-at-ottawa-little-theatre-05-15-18/ title=Preview: Doug Stanhope at Ottawa Little Theatre—05.15.18 publisher=Apt613 first=Asim last=B date=May 9, 2018 accessdate=18 July 2018}}</ref> He developed his act in Las Vegas before he moved to Phoenix, Arizona, where he landed a gig as a house MC at a comedy club.<ref name=beast/><ref name=guardian2011/> He also lived in Florida, Oceanside, California, Idaho, and at one point returned to Massachusetts.<ref name=apt/> Stanhope lived out of his car for three years.

In 1995, he settled in West Hollywood which he "hated [...] so much" partly due to the slump in comedy acts at the time.<ref name=beast/> Among his regular spots were the Hollywood Improv or The Comedy Store, before he became based in Playa del Rey and worked in clubs outside the city center.<ref name=beast/> Around this time, he landed a development deal and "flirted" with the idea of producing a sitcom by co-writing a pilot episode with a writer of The Mary Tyler Moore Show.<ref name=beast/> He later described the script as a "piece of shit", and felt thankful it was never picked up.<ref name=beast/> In 1995, Stanhope won the San Francisco International Comedy Competition against Dane Cook in a three-week contest.<ref>{{Cite web url=http://sanfranciscocomedycompetition.com/articles/great-dane-took-a-bite-out-of-the-competition-in-1995  title=Great Dane took a bite out of the Competition in 1995  author=Globie  publisher=San Francisco Comedy Competition  date=2007-12-07  deadurl=yes  archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20140221212650/http://sanfranciscocomedycompetition.com/articles/great-dane-took-a-bite-out-of-the-competition-in-1995  archivedate=February 21, 2014 }}</ref>

===2000s===
In the aftermath of the September 11, 2001 attacks in New York City, Stanhope received some criticism from audiences over his comments regarding the event. This included protests outside of his gigs and attacks on stage.<ref name=independent2004/>

In July 2002, Fox aired back-to-back episodes of Stanhopes hidden camera television show Invasion of the Hidden Cameras.<ref name=globe2002/> The project began in 1999, with Stanhope writing and producing various pranks for it, before the network decided to withdraw its broadcast.<ref name=globe2002>{{cite web url=https://www.highbeam.com/doc/1P2-7725579.html newspaper=The Boston Globe title=Off the shelf at last date=July 12, 2002 via=Highbeam Research subscription=yes accessdate=July 18, 2018}}</ref> Stanhope accused Spy TV of stealing half of his ideas and achieving good ratings as a result.<ref name=globe2002/> He starred as one of the pranksters on Spy TV.

In August 2002, Stanhope won the Strathmore Press Award for his week-long run at the Edinburgh Fringe Edinburgh Festival Fringe, as voted by 40 press critics. The prize was £1,000 and a feature in the British celebrity magazine OK!.<ref>{{cite web title=Stanhope is critics choice url=http://www.chortle.co.uk/news/2002/08/22/1623/stanhope_is_critics_choice publisher=Chortle.co.uk date=August 22, 2002}}</ref> Following the run, Stanhope signed with his longtime manager Brian Hennigan.<ref>{{cite web url=https://www.highbeam.com/doc/1P2-13001455.html title=Fringe stars opt for local management talent newspaper=The Scotsman first=Mike last=Wade date=26 November 2002 via=Highbeam Research subscription=yes accessdate=18 July 2018}}</ref>

In 2003 and 2004, Stanhope co-hosted the List of The Man Show episodes#Season 5 fifth and List of The Man Show episodes#Season 6 sixth seasons of The Man Show with Joe Rogan. He agreed to the project in order to become more financially stable.<ref name=guardian2006/> Among the shows segments included Stanhope lasting five rounds in a boxing match with Tonya Harding, and his mother reviewing pornographic films.<ref name=independent2004/> He hosted his own radio show on SIRIUS Satellite Radio in 2005.<ref>{{Cite web url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GvDvMDqSvIg title=Doug Stanhope Live at Leeds pt 2/2 publisher=YouTube date=2010-01-19 accessdate=2010-08-13}}</ref> That year, Stanhope hosted Girls Gone Wild: America Uncovered.<ref>[https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=2xAO28e-jT8 Doug Stanhope Trashes Girls Gone Wild Joe Francis! Pt. 1"], youtube.com; accessed November 29, 2015.</ref>

Stanhope established a group of touring comics known as The Unbookables featuring such artists as Andy Andrist, James Inman, and Brendon Walsh, among others.<ref>{{Cite web url=https://www.imdb.com/title/tt1379206 title=Unbookables on IMDb accessdate=2009-06-19}}</ref> The Unbookables first CD, Morbid Obscenity.

In 2005, Stanhope moved to Bisbee, Arizona.<ref name=beast/>

He appeared in the film The Aristocrats (film) The Aristocrats, telling a caustic joke to a baby.

In June 2006, Stanhope was booked to perform several shows at the Cat Laughs Cat Laughs Comedy Festival in Kilkenny, Ireland. However, roughly ten minutes into his set, a joke about Irish men committing pedophilia because of the ugliness of Irish women for raping caused a hostile reaction from the audience. The incident led to the cancellation of Stanhopes remaining appearances at the festival.<ref name=guardian2006>{{cite news url=https://www.theguardian.com/stage/2006/jul/31/comedy.edinburgh2006 title=If Youre Offended, Then Youre Not Listening work=The Guardian date=July 31, 2006 accessdate=September 5, 2006 location=London, UK first=Brian last=Logan}}</ref> That August, he returned to the Edinburgh Festival Fringe, agreeing to a full three-week residency.<ref name=guardian2006/> At one show, one of his routines sparked a violent affray with an angered audience member and the police were called in, causing Stanhope to leave the venue.<ref name=statesman>{{cite journal url=https://www.highbeam.com/doc/1P3-1147007911.html title=The heir to Bill Hicks date=2 October 2006 magazine=New Statesman first=Brian last=Logan accessdate=18 July 2018 via=Highbeam Research subscription=yes}}</ref> He gained 5-star reviews from the press. On his opening night he took what was believed to be an MDMA ecstasy tablet that was handed to him by a member of the audience.<ref>"[http://entertainment.timesonline.co.uk/article/0,,23229-2315695,00.html "Itll Blow Your Mind"], The Sunday Times, August 20, 2006; retrieved September 5, 2006.</ref><ref>[http://enjoyment.independent.co.uk/theatre/reviews/article1218522.ece "Boozy and Brilliant"] {{webarchive  url=https://web.archive.org/web/20060813061428/http://enjoyment.independent.co.uk/theatre/reviews/article1218522.ece  date=August 13, 2006 }}, The Independent, August 11, 2006; retrieved September 5, 2006.</ref> At the 2008 Fringe, Stanhope announced A Day with Doug, an opportunity to spend an entire day with him for £7,349, the average cost that comedians lose when they are booked at one of the four biggest venues at the festival.<ref>{{cite newspaper url=https://www.highbeam.com/doc/1P2-17033894.html title=Cheaper laughs from Stanhope date=18 August 2008 newspaper=The Scotsman first=Tim last=Cornwell accessdate=18 July 2018 via=Highbeam Research subscription=yes}}</ref>

Stanhope announced his intention to seek election as the United States President in the 2008 election on the Libertarian Party (United States) Libertarian Party ticket. He added: "It seemed like a funny thing to do [...] But people were either amused or horrified at the idea of me representing their party".<ref name=guardian2011/> His focus was for "individual freedom, self-government and making America fun again".<ref name=guardian2006/>

His live show was placed in the top 5 of the 20 Best Live Shows of 2009 by Londons The Guardian newspaper.<ref>{{cite news author=Brian Logan url=https://www.theguardian.com/stage/2009/jul/27/comedy-2009-best-live-shows title=20 best comedy live shows for summer/autumn 2009 publisher=The Guardian date=July 27, 2009 accessdate=2010-08-13 location=London, UK}}</ref>

Stanhopes seventh album, From Across The Street, was released on November 24, 2009.

===2010s===
In 2010, Stanhope recorded a series of vignettes for the British news show Newswipe with Charlie Brooker.<ref name=guardian2011/> In 2013, he did the same during Charlie Brookers Weekly Wipe as "the voice of America".<ref name=esquire2013/> On November 17, 2010, Stanhope signed to rock and metal label Roadrunner Records to launch their new comedy label, Roadrunner Comedy. On May 3, 2011, the album was released: Oslo: Burning The Bridge To Nowhere.<ref>{{cite web url=http://www.roadrunnerrecords.com/news/Doug-Stanhope-To-Release-Roadrunner-Records-Comedy-Debut-22779.aspx title=Doug Stanhope To Release Roadrunner Records Comedy Debut! date=March 8, 2011}}</ref><ref>{{cite web url=http://www.iconvsicon.com/2011/03/08/doug-stanhope-to-release-oslo-burning-the-bridge-to-nowhere-in-may title=Doug Stanhope To Release Oslo: Burning The Bridge To Nowhere in May date=March 8, 2011}}</ref> This was supported with an international tour, which included a 25-night residency at the Leicester Square Theatre in London.<ref name=portions>{{cite web archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20110711154852/http://www.suchsmallportions.com/pg/news/suchsmallportions/read/31316/doug-stanhope-interview-guardian-people-fuck-them url=http://www.suchsmallportions.com/pg/news/suchsmallportions/read/31316/doug-stanhope-interview-guardian-people-fuck-them title=Comedy news: Doug Stanhope interview: "Guardian people? Fuck them" date=6 July 2011 publisher=Such Small Portions archivedate=July 11, 2011 accessdate=18 July 2018}}</ref> By this time, Stanhope had won Time Out New York{{s}} Best Comedy Performance award twice.<ref>{{cite magazine url=https://www.highbeam.com/doc/1G1-255493581.html title=Defending Charlie Sheen magazine=Reason date=1 June 2011 via=Highbeam Research subscription=yes accessdate=21 July 2018}}</ref>

In 2011, in co-operation with the mayor of Reykjavík and comedian Jón Gnarr, Stanhope scheduled a performance in Icelands only maximum security prison, Litla-Hraun. The show took place on September 25. Also in 2011, Stanhope appeared on the FX (TV channel) FX series Louie (U.S. TV series) Louie as Eddie Mack, a comedian that Louis C.K. knew 20 years earlier when they first started performing, in the season 2 episode entitled "Eddie", first aired on August 11, 2011. He spoke of his role: "I tried to beg out of it. I said, I suck at acting. He had to talk me into trying", and auditioned online through Skype.<ref name=beast/><ref name="imdb">{{IMDb name id=0970268 name=Doug Stanhope}}</ref>

In 2012, Stanhope released his comedy special Before Turning the Gun on Himself, recorded in Salt Lake City. He named it after how commonplace the phrase was on online news articles.<ref name=inept>{{cite web url=http://theineptowl.com/2016/09/17/party-member-doug-stanhope-interview/ title=Not a Party Member: Doug Stanhope Interview date=September 17, 2016 first=Rick last=Bernardo publisher=The Inept Owl accessdate=18 July 2018}}</ref> Also in 2012, he impersonated John Lydon in a pre-taped radio interview with Mike Ragogna after the studio mistakenly called his number thinking it was Lydons.<ref>{{cite web url=https://www.punknews.org/article/49494/doug-stanhope-impersonates-john-lydon-for-huffington-post-interview title=Doug Stanhope impersonates John Lydon for Huffington Post interview publisher=Punk News first=Aubin last=Paul accessdate=18 July 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite web archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20121024140541/http://www.huffingtonpost.com/mike-ragogna/chatting-with-pils-john-l_b_2004088.html url=http://www.huffingtonpost.com/mike-ragogna/chatting-with-pils-john-l_b_2004088.html title=Chatting With PiLs John Lydon, Bad Books Kevin Devine, and Lord Hurons Ben Schneider, Plus New Unknown Component Video date=October 23, 2012 first=Mike last=Ragogna work=Huffington Post accessdate=July 18, 2018 archivedate=October 24, 2012}}</ref>

On February 16, 2013, Stanhope debuted The Doug Stanhope Podcast, recorded out of his home in Bisbee, Arizona. In 2014, it was hosted by the All Things Comedy network.<ref name=LS>{{cite web last1=Procida first1=Billy title=Doug Stanhope’s Podcast joins Bill Burr, Al Madrigals All Things Comedy podcast network url=http://www.laughspin.com/2014/05/20/doug-stanhopes-podcast-joins-bill-burr-al-madrigals-all-things-comedy-podcast-network/ publisher=Laughspin.com accessdate=October 3, 2014 deadurl=yes archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20141006100218/http://www.laughspin.com/2014/05/20/doug-stanhopes-podcast-joins-bill-burr-al-madrigals-all-things-comedy-podcast-network/ archivedate=October 6, 2014 df=}}</ref>

In May 2013, a fundraiser initiated by Stanhope raised nearly $126,000 for a woman and her family whose home was destroyed by the 2013 Moore tornado 2013 Moore, Oklahoma tornado. He said he did so in support of her declared atheism, saying he admired her courage in a predominantly Christian state.<ref name=phoenix201305>{{cite web url=http://www.phoenixnewtimes.com/news/doug-stanhope-raising-thousands-for-tornado-survior-who-told-wolf-blitzer-shes-an-atheist-6662560 title=Doug Stanhope Raising Thousands for Tornado Survior Who Told Wolf Blitzer Shes an Atheist first=Matthew last=Hendley date=29 May 2013 work=Phoenix New Times accessdate=18 July 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite web last=Stanhope first= Doug  date=October 17, 2013 url= https://www.vice.com/en_us/article/the-problem-with-charity  title=The Problem with Charity work=Vice (magazine) Vice}}</ref><ref>{{cite news last=Lulppold first= Ross url=http://www.huffingtonpost.com/2013/12/31/doug-stanhope-raises-money-for-atheist-out-of-spite_n_4524264.html publisher=The Huffington Post title=Atheist Comedian Lends Helping Hand As A Big F*ck You date=December 31, 2013}}</ref>

On May 5, 2014, Stanhope announced on The Howard Stern Show that he was working with Johnny Depp on a show. According to Stanhope, Depp befriended him in Europe and had an unknown project in mind for him.<ref>[https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8sGrBKjN8Ds YouTube]; retrieved May 13, 2015.</ref> He had a supporting role in the 2014 Chris Rock film Top Five, playing a police officer.<ref name=esquire2013/><ref>{{cite web url=http://azstarnet.com/entertainment/blogs/caliente-tuned-in/doug-stanhope-hosts-free-comedy-show/article_2244a7d7-5e0b-5774-ac4f-2e37c9f48118.html title=National comedian Stanhope sneaks into Tucson accessdate=October 3, 2015}}</ref>

Doug appeared on Bar Rescue as Jon Taffers guest to watch the recon of the Pomona, California bar Friar Tucks in an episode called "List of Bar Rescue episodes#Season 4 .282014.E2.80.9316.29 Storming the Castle".

In May 2015, Doug announced that he was writing a book about his life with his mother, an occasional subject in his comedy.<ref>[http://www.dougstanhope.com/journal/2015/5/16/im-writing-a-book "Im writing a book"], dougstanhope.com, May 16, 2015.</ref> The book, entitled Digging Up Mother: A Love Story, was released on May 10, 2016 and includes a foreword by Johnny Depp.<ref name=deadline/><ref>Stanhope, Doug (2016). In June 2016, actress Amber Heard sued Stanhope for his "defamatory" claim that she was blackmailing her ex-husband Johnny Depp.<ref>{{cite news title=Amber Heard sues Doug Stanhope for defamation, alleges Johnny Depp ‘suffocated’ her once author=Meera Jagannathan work=Daily News (New York) Daily News  location= New York City date=June 3, 2016 url=http://www.nydailynews.com/entertainment/gossip/heard-sues-stanhope-defamation-claims-depp-suffocated-article-1.2660430 accessdate=June 3, 2016  archivedate=June 4, 2016  archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20160604132454/http://www.nydailynews.com/entertainment/gossip/heard-sues-stanhope-defamation-claims-depp-suffocated-article-1.2660430   deadurl=no}}</ref> Heard dropped the lawsuit in the aftermath of her and Depps $7 million divorce settlement.<ref name=beast>{{cite web url=https://www.thedailybeast.com/articles/2017/04/02/you-re-all-f-cking-industry-tools-the-ballad-of-doug-stanhope-a-stand-up-scumbag title=Youre All F*cking Industry Tools: The Ballad of Doug Stanhope, a Stand-Up Scumbag date=2 April 2017 first=Matt last=Wilstein publisher=The Daily Beast accessdate=18 July 2018}}</ref>

==Political activism==
Although his views have led people to believe that he is a Modern liberalism in the United States liberal, he does not accept this characterization. Stanhope says that libertarianism was a phase he went through, but he now describes himself as an Anarchism anarchist.<ref>{{Cite web  url=http://www.list.co.uk/article/40464-interview-doug-stanhope-on-politics  title=Interview: Doug Stanhope on politics  last=Boyle  first=Niki  date=February 16, 2012  publisher=List.co.uk  access-date=November 23, 2017}}</ref><ref>{{Cite tweet  number=933561532109094912  user=DougStanhope  title=Libertarian was a phase I went through.  author=Doug Stanhope  date=November 23, 2017}}</ref> In 2004, he endorsed the Free State Project, a proposed political migration of at least 20,000 libertarians to a single low-population state, in order to make the state a stronghold for libertarian ideas. According to Stanhope this is an idea "that could produce tangible change in our lifetime."<ref>{{Cite web url=http://freestateproject.org/about/endorsements#stanhope title=Doug Stanhopes endorsement of the Free State Project accessdate=2008-08-31}}</ref> In 2012, Stanhope originally supported Republican Party (United States) Republican Ron Paul presidential campaign, 2012 Ron Paul for the United States presidential election, 2012 2012 presidential election, but later endorsed Libertarian Party (United States) Libertarian Gary Johnson presidential campaign, 2012 Gary Johnson.<ref>{{cite web url=http://www.dougstanhope.com/journal/2012/9/12/presidents-apocalypse-and-death.html title=Presidents, Apocalypse, and Death work=DougStanhope.com date=September 12, 2012 accessdate=November 6, 2012}}</ref>

==Personal life==
In 2002, Stanhope and his girlfriend Renee Morrison staged a marriage ceremony in Las Vegas. He later said that it was "mocking the institution of marriage", and that it was not legal.<ref name=punchline2007>{{cite web archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20070928042825/http://www.punchlinemagazine.com/index.php?page=185 url=http://www.punchlinemagazine.com/index.php?page=185 archivedate=28 September 2007 date=2007 title=Doug Stanhope: Out of Darkness Comes Comedy work=Punchline Magazine accessdate=20 July 2018}}</ref> At one point, the two were expecting a child but opted for abortion, and Stanhope got a vasectomy.<ref name=independent2004/> Stanhope dated comedian and former attorney Betsy Wise for 18 months.<ref name=independent2004/> Around 2005 Stanhope met his current partner, Amy "Bingo" Bingerman who he has referred to as his girlfriend and wife.<ref name=beast/><ref name=guardian2011/> Stanhope is opposed to having children and cites overpopulation as a reason.<ref>{{cite web url=http://thelaughbutton.com/features/burning-bridges-interview-doug-stanhope  title=Burning Bridges: An Interview with Doug Stanhope date=April 29, 2011 accessdate= September 30, 2017 publisher=TheLaughButton.com archivedate= September 30, 2017 archiveurl=https://archive.is/20170930192351/http://thelaughbutton.com/features/burning-bridges-interview-doug-stanhope/ deadurl=no}}</ref>

Stanhope is known for his heavy alcohol consumption and is a self-confessed drunk.<ref name=independent2004>{{cite web url=https://www.independent.co.uk/news/people/profiles/doug-stanhope-on-the-offensive-5384584.html title=Doug Stanhope: On the offensive first=Robert last=Chalmers date=28 March 2004 work=The Guardian accessdate=18 July 2018}}</ref><ref name=guardian2006/> In a 2011 interview, he said the last time he was sober on stage was in 2003.<ref name=portions/> He is also known for dressing in thrift store clothing.<ref name=inept/> He describes himself as pro-choice and pro-drug.<ref name=guardian2011/>
