==Early life==
Notaro was born in Jackson, Mississippi. Her mother was born in New Orleans.<ref name=NOLA-BoyishGirl-2014/> Notaro was raised in Pass Christian, Mississippi, until kindergarten. Her family later moved to Spring, Texas, a suburb of Houston, Texas Houston.<ref name=MotherJones-Interview-2013>{{cite news last1=Brownstone first1=Sydney title=Tig Notaro: Youll Laugh, Youll Cry  url=https://www.motherjones.com/media/2013/04/interview-tig-notaro-cancer-professor-blastoff-inside-amy-schumer accessdate=December 1, 2014 work=Mother Jones Magazine Mother Jones issue=May/June 2013}}</ref><ref name=BuzzFeed-Year-2013>{{cite news last1=Allen first1=Sandra title=Tig Notaro And The Terrible, Horrible, No Good, Very Bad, Yet Somehow Completely Amazing Year url=https://www.buzzfeed.com/sandraeallen/tig-notaro-and-the-terrible-horrible-no-good-very-bad-yet-so accessdate=December 1, 2014 work=BuzzFeed date=August 9, 2013}}</ref> She has a brother, Renaud Notaro, who is a year older than she and is a radio talk show host.<ref name=LarryKingNow-Interview-2014/><ref name=Earwolf-Renaud>{{cite web title=Person: Renaud Notaro url=http://www.earwolf.com/person/renaud-notaro website=Earwolf accessdate=December 1, 2014}}</ref>

Notaros great-great-grandfather was John Fitzpatrick (New Orleans) John Fitzpatrick, mayor of New Orleans from 1892 to 1896.<ref name=NOLA-BoyishGirl-2014>{{cite news last1=Root first1=Cate title=Tig Notaro brings Boyish Girl Interrupted tour to New Orleans Oct. 12 url=http://www.nola.com/arts/index.ssf/2014/10/tig_notaro_new_orleans.html accessdate=December 1, 2014 work=The Times-Picayune date=October 7, 2014}}</ref><ref name="GambitWeekly-Interview-2011">{{cite news first=Will last=Coviello url=http://www.bestofneworleans.com/gambit/big-name-talent/Content?oid=1495833 title=Interview: Tig Notaro work=The Gambit Weekly date=January 3, 2011 accessdate=May 14, 2012}}</ref>

Notaro said she hated school. She failed three grades, eventually dropping out of high school. In 1990, while living in Texas, Notaro got her General Equivalency Diploma GED.<ref name=MotherJones-Interview-2013/><ref name=Netflix-Tig-2015>{{cite web last1=Goolsby first1=Kristina last2=York first2=Ashley title=Tig url=https://www.netflix.com/title/80028208 website=Netflix accessdate=17 July 2015 date=17 July 2015}}</ref>

==Career==
File:Tig Notaro 2010.jpg upright right thumb Tig Notaro in 2010
Notaro moved to Denver, Colorado, where she became involved in the music industry. She became a band manager, working under the name Tignation Promotions in the mid-90s.<ref name=Magnet-DontRush-2011>{{cite news last1=Rapa first1=Patrick date=September 14, 2011 url=http://www.magnetmagazine.com/2011/09/14/tig-notaro-dont-rush-me title=Tig Notaro: Dont Rush Me work=Magnet (magazine) Magnet accessdate=October 14, 2012}}</ref>

Notaros work promoting bands took her to Los Angeles, where she tried stand-up for the first time in the late 90s.<ref name=MotherJones-Interview-2013/><ref>{{Cite news url=https://www.vanityfair.com/culture/2013/01/tig-notaro-breast-cancer-dont-connect-with-jokes title=Tig Notaro on How Surviving Cancer Changes Humor: "I Dont Connect With Some of My Jokes Anymore" last=Grigoriadis first=Vanessa work=Vanity Fair access-date=2017-12-28 language=en}}</ref> Notaro has since been featured on Comedy Central Presents and on The Sarah Silverman Program as a lesbian police officer. She collaborates frequently with writing partner Kyle Dunnigan. With Dunnigan and David Huntsberger, she co-hosted the podcast Professor Blastoff from May 2011 until July 2015.

In 2011, she released her debut stand-up album, Good One. Her 2012 sophomore album, Live (Tig Notaro album) Live, is a recording of a stand-up set performed shortly after she was diagnosed with breast cancer.

In 2012, Notaro appeared on the Conan (talk show) Conan television show, and in May of that year on the live episode of This American Life, which was broadcast to theaters nationwide and on radio in edited form. She performed a monologue about having encountered Taylor Dayne on multiple occasions, greeting her each time with, "Excuse me, Im sorry to bother you, but I just have to tell you. I love your voice."  After her monologue, Dayne made a surprise appearance, serenading Notaro with the song "Ill Always Love You".<ref>{{cite web url=http://www.thisamericanlife.org/blog/2012/05/live-show-redux  title=Live Show Redux  publisher=This American Life  date=2012-05-18  accessdate=2012-05-22}}</ref> She worked on fellow comedian Amy Schumers Comedy Central series Inside Amy Schumer. Also in 2012, Notaro did a Kickstarter-funded series called Clown Service written by and starring Notaro.<ref name=Kickstarter-ClownService-2014>{{cite web title=Clown Service url=https://www.kickstarter.com/projects/tignotaro/clown-service website=Kickstarter accessdate=22 August 2015}}</ref>

Notaro wrote a memoir for HarperCollins imprint Ecco Press Ecco called Im Just a Person,<ref name=Guardian-TragedyGold-2012>{{cite news last1=Holland first1=Jessica title=How Tig Notaro turned tragedy into comic gold url=https://www.theguardian.com/culture/2012/oct/21/tig-notaro-interview-cancer-standup accessdate=February 23, 2015 work=The Guardian date=October 20, 2012}}</ref> and there is a Showtime (TV network) Showtime documentary<ref name=HRerporter-ShowDoc-2013>{{cite news last1=Goldberg first1=Lesley title=Showtime Sets Tig Notaro Documentary url=http://www.hollywoodreporter.com/live-feed/showtime-sets-tig-notaro-documentary-578643 accessdate=December 1, 2014 work=The Hollywood Reporter date=July 2, 2013}}</ref> made about her life that chronicled her post-cancer stand-up tour<ref name=LarryKingNow-Interview-2014/> called Knock Knock, Its Tig Notaro.<ref name=Showtime-KnockKnock-2015>{{cite web title=Knock Knock, Its Tig Notaro url=http://www.sho.com/sho/comedy/titles/3401415/knock-knock-its-tig-notaro#/index website=Showtime (TV network) Showtime accessdate=17 July 2015}}</ref>

===Comedic style===
On her approach to comedy (and whether she considers herself a dark comic): "Im always going to do whatever I think is funniest. If somethings dark, Ill do it. If its a sock puppet, if its a stool, Ill do it. Theres no preconceived idea of who I think I might be now."<ref name=MotherJones-Interview-2013/> Notaro said that since her cancer diagnosis, she has shifted not to darker comedy but rather to personal comedy. Previously she was more distant and Observational comedy observational, but now she reflects on her childhood and her life.<ref name=LarryKingNow-Interview-2014/>

In the autumn of 2016, she appeared in video as an onstage "stand-in" during the Nostalgic for the Present Tour Nostalgia For the Present concert tour of Australian singer Sia Furler for the song "Diamonds (Rihanna song) Diamonds."<ref>{{cite web last1=Stevenson first1=Jane title=Crowd eats up pop star Sias minimalistic show url=http://www.torontosun.com/2016/10/23/crowd-eats-up-pop-star-sias-minimalistic-show website=Torontosun.com accessdate=6 November 2016}}</ref>

=== Cancer ===
Notaro was diagnosed with cancer in both breasts on July 30, 2012.<ref name=HuffPo-Cancer-2012>{{cite news  work=The Huffington Post url=http://www.huffingtonpost.com/2012/08/04/tig-notaro-breast-cancer_n_1742071.html title=Tig Notaro Has Breast Cancer: Comedian Announces Illness On Podcast One Day After Diagnosis date=August 4, 2012 accessdate=February 23, 2015}}</ref> On August 3, she addressed her recent cancer diagnosis and other personal difficulties during her live stage show at Largo (nightclub) Largo in Los Angeles.<ref name=FreshAir-Live-2013>{{cite news last1=Gross first1=Terry last2=Bianculli first2=David title=Tig Notaro On Going Live About Her Life url=https://www.npr.org/2013/07/26/205540155/tig-notaro-on-going-live-about-her-life accessdate=December 1, 2014 work=Fresh Air publisher=NPR.org date=July 26, 2013 format=Audio interview}}</ref> The set has been described as "instantly legendary," with many comedians praising her work.<ref name=RollingStone-Live-2012>{{cite web title=Louis C.K.
